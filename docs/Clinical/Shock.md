# Shock

*Last updated: Mar 21, 2026*

---

## Shock Definition

- **Definition:** Inadequate Tissue Perfusion to Meet End-Organ Needs
- Cellular/Tissue Hypoxia from Reduced Oxygen Delivery, Increased Oxygen Consumption, or Inadequate Oxygen Utilization
- Generally Caused by Circulatory Failure with Hypotension
    - Not Based Solely on Vital Signs
    - Can Present with Hypertension in Certain Cases

***In other words: VO₂ (oxygen consumption) > DO₂ (oxygen delivery)***

---

### DO₂ / VO₂ Curve

| | |
|---|---|
| **Shock State** (left of Critical DO₂) | **Normal State** (right of Critical DO₂) |
| Cellular VO₂ is **dependent on DO₂**. Cells would consume more oxygen, but there is no more being delivered. These cells depend on **anaerobic metabolism** and are producing lactate and will eventually die. | Cellular VO₂ is **independent of DO₂**. These cells depend on aerobic metabolism and are not producing lactate. Moving leftward on this curve represents either an increase in cellular VO₂ or a decrease in DO₂. |

```
        "Critical DO₂"
              ↓
VO₂  |       /‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾‾
     |      /
     |     /
     |    /
     |___/
              DO₂
```

---

### Core Formula

$$VO_2 > CO \times CaO_2$$

---

## Shock Classifications

**Distributive Shock** — Severe Peripheral Vasodilation *(50% — Most Common)*

- *Septic Shock*
- *Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (SIRS)*
- *Neurogenic Shock*
- *Anaphylactic Shock*
- *Endocrine Shock (Addisonian/Adrenal Crisis)*
- *Drug/Toxin-Induced Shock*

**Cardiogenic Shock** — Cardiac Etiology Causing Reduced Cardiac Output *(14%)*

**Hypovolemic Shock** — From Reduced Intravascular Volume *(31%)*

- *Hemorrhagic Shock*
- *Non-Hemorrhagic Hypovolemic Shock*

**Obstructive Shock** — Extra-Cardiac Etiology of Cardiac Pump Failure *(1%)*

## Treatment Principles

The treatment of shock has three objectives:

1. Increase DO₂
2. Decrease VO₂
3. Treat the underlying cause

---

### Full Treatment Framework

$$VO_2 > \underbrace{(HR \times SV)}_{\text{CO}} \times \underbrace{(1.34 \times Hgb \times SaO_2 + 0.003 \times PaO_2)}_{CaO_2}$$

**Decrease VO₂ (O₂ Consumption):**

- Sedation
- Analgesia
- Paralysis
- PPV
- Treat seizures
- Treat arrhythmias
- Treat fever

**Increase CO (HR × SV):**

| Component | Intervention |
|---|---|
| **HR** | Increase chronotropy, increase dromotropy, treat arrhythmias |
| **Preload (EDV)** | Vasoconstrictors, intravascular bolus |
| **Afterload (ESV)** | Vasodilators, PPV |
| **Contractility** | Inotropic agents, temperature, normal pH, normal electrolytes, remove toxins |

**Increase CaO₂:**

- pRBC transfusion
- Increase FiO₂, adequate ventilation
- Maintain PEEP, optimize Qp:Qs
- Avoid alteration in Hgb O₂ offloading
