# Sepsis

*Last updated: Apr 2026*

---

## Quick Reference

### Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Sepsis-1** | ≥2 SIRS criteria + suspected infection |
| **Severe Sepsis** | Sepsis + organ dysfunction or tissue hypoperfusion |
| **Septic Shock (old)** | Hypoperfusion despite fluids OR lactate ≥4.0 mmol/L |
| **Sepsis-3 (current)** | Life-threatening organ dysfunction from dysregulated host response to infection (SOFA ↑≥2) |
| **Septic Shock (Sepsis-3)** | Sepsis + vasopressor-dependent hypotension + lactate >2 mmol/L without hypovolemia |

---

### SIRS Criteria

≥2 of the following:

| Criterion | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Temperature | >38°C or <36°C |
| Heart Rate | >90 bpm |
| Respiratory Rate | >20 breaths/min or PaCO₂ <32 mmHg |
| WBC | >12,000/µL or <4,000/µL or >10% bands |

---

### qSOFA

≥2 of the following = high risk for poor outcome:

| Criterion | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Systolic BP | ≤100 mmHg |
| Respiratory Rate | ≥22 breaths/min |
| Altered Mental Status | GCS <15 |

---

### SOFA Score

Each category scored 0–4 (total 0–24). An increase of ≥2 from baseline = organ dysfunction.

| System | Parameter | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Respiratory** | PaO₂/FiO₂ | <400 | <300 | <200 + vent | <100 + vent |
| **Coagulation** | Platelets (×10³/µL) | <150 | <100 | <50 | <20 |
| **Liver** | Bilirubin (mg/dL) | 1.2–1.9 | 2.0–5.9 | 6.0–11.9 | ≥12 |
| **Cardiovascular** | MAP or vasopressors | MAP <70 | Dopamine ≤5 or Dobutamine | Dopamine >5 or NE/Epi ≤0.1 | Dopamine >15 or NE/Epi >0.1 |
| **CNS** | GCS | 13–14 | 10–12 | 6–9 | <6 |
| **Renal** | Creatinine (mg/dL) or UO | 1.2–1.9 | 2.0–3.4 | 3.5–4.9 or <500 mL/day | ≥5 or <200 mL/day |

!!! warning "Mortality"
    Initial SOFA score >11 carries ~95% mortality.

### End-Organ Dysfunction Criteria (CMS)

- SBP <90 or MAP <70 mmHg
- Creatinine >2.0 mg/dL or UOP <0.5 mL/kg/hr for >2 hours
- Bilirubin >2 mg/dL
- Platelets <100,000
- Coagulopathy: INR >1.5 or aPTT >60 seconds
- Lactate >2 mmol/L

---

### Disposition

| Hemodynamics | Lactate | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| Normotensive | ≤2 mmol/L | Floor |
| Normotensive | 2.0–3.5 mmol/L | MICU consult |
| Hypotensive OR | ≥3.5 mmol/L | ICU |
| Vasopressor-dependent OR | ≥4 mmol/L | ICU |

## Overview

Sepsis is a **life-threatening organ dysfunction** caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. It exists on a spectrum — from infection alone to septic shock — and carries an in-hospital mortality of ~15%, rising to 40–50% in septic shock.

**Risk Factors:**

- Advanced age (>65 years)
- Nursing home / long-term care residence
- Comorbidities: diabetes, CHF, COPD, liver disease, cancer, CKD
- Immunosuppression: chemotherapy, chronic steroids, immunomodulators

**Common Sources:**

- Pneumonia
- UTI
- Central line infection
- Skin / soft tissue infection

---

## Initial Actions and Primary Survey

!!! tip "High Yield"
    Time-sensitive — don't wait for a perfect diagnosis. Treat empirically while working up the source.

**Immediate Steps:**

1. Obtain IV access (×2 large bore) and place on continuous monitoring
2. Draw **lactate** (venous acceptable) — must be a fresh sample
3. Draw **2 sets of blood cultures** from 2 different sites before antibiotics
4. Obtain source-directed cultures (UA/urine culture, sputum, wound, etc.)
5. Administer **broad-spectrum antibiotics** as early as possible
6. Begin **30 mL/kg IV crystalloid** (Lactated Ringers preferred) for hypotension or lactate ≥4 mmol/L
7. Reassess frequently — patients can decompensate **30–60 minutes after antibiotics**

---

## Presentation

**Classic:**

- Fever, tachycardia, tachypnea, hypotension, altered mental status

**Atypical (do not miss):**

- Malaise, fatigue, weakness
- Mild delirium with inattention
- Patient may appear well initially — especially elderly and immunocompromised

!!! warning "High-Risk Populations"
    Elderly, immunocompromised, and nursing home patients may present without fever or leukocytosis. Maintain high suspicion.

## Diagnostic Testing

**Labs:**

| Test | Purpose |
|---|---|
| CBC with differential | Leukocytosis, leukopenia, bandemia |
| BMP / CMP | Renal and hepatic dysfunction, glucose |
| Lactate (venous) | Tissue hypoperfusion, risk stratification |
| Blood cultures ×2 | Before antibiotics — identify organism |
| Urinalysis + urine culture | UTI as source |
| Coagulation studies (INR, aPTT) | DIC screening |
| LFTs | Hepatic dysfunction |

**Lactate Interpretation:**

| Level | Significance |
|---|---|
| ≤2 mmol/L | Normal |
| 2–4 mmol/L | Elevated — repeat after resuscitation |
| ≥4 mmol/L | Severely elevated — aggressive resuscitation, ICU |

Lactate represents **anaerobic metabolism** during inadequate oxygen delivery. Elevated levels correlate directly with mortality.

**Imaging:**

- CXR (PA/lateral if stable, portable if critical) — rule out pneumonia
- CT abdomen/pelvis if abdominal source suspected
- Additional imaging guided by clinical presentation

## Treatment

### Antibiotics

- Start **within 1 hour** of recognition
- Guide selection by suspected source and institutional antibiogram
- Obtain cultures first, but do **not delay antibiotics** for cultures

### Fluid Resuscitation

- **30 mL/kg IV Lactated Ringers** for hypotension or lactate ≥4 mmol/L
- Reassess after each fluid challenge
- Avoid in pulmonary edema or cardiogenic shock

### Vasopressors

- Start if MAP remains <65 after fluids
- **First line: Norepinephrine** (adults)
- Dopamine: alternative in select cases (infants/children)

### CMS SEP-1 Bundles

**3-Hour Bundle:**

- [ ] Measure lactate
- [ ] Blood cultures before antibiotics
- [ ] Broad-spectrum antibiotics
- [ ] 30 mL/kg LR for hypotension or lactate ≥4

**6-Hour Bundle:**

- [ ] Vasopressors for refractory hypotension (target MAP ≥65)
- [ ] Reassess volume status
- [ ] Repeat lactate if initial ≥4 mmol/L

**Surviving Sepsis Hour-1 Bundle (2018):**

- Measure lactate (remeasure if elevated)
- Blood cultures before antibiotics
- Broad-spectrum antibiotics
- 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate ≥4
- Vasopressors if hypotensive during or after resuscitation

!!! note "Controversy"
    The Hour-1 Bundle was initially rejected by ACEP and SCCM. ProCESS, ARISE, and ProMISe trials found no mortality difference between EGDT, standardized care, and routine care. CVP and ScvO₂ monitoring are no longer emphasized.

## Pearls and Pitfalls

!!! tip "Pearls"
    - Maintain high suspicion in elderly, immunocompromised, and nursing home patients
    - A fresh lactate sample is essential — do not use old draws
    - Blood cultures must be from **two separate sites** before antibiotics
    - Reassess frequently — decompensation can happen 30–60 min after initial treatment
    - Use your institution's screening tools and order sets
    - Lactate clearance (targeting ≤2 mmol/L or 10% reduction) is non-inferior to EGDT

!!! danger "Pitfalls"
    - Anchoring on a "benign" presentation — sepsis can look subtle
    - Delaying antibiotics waiting for cultures or imaging
    - Giving 30 mL/kg fluids to a patient with pulmonary edema or cardiogenic shock
    - Missing atypical presentations in elderly patients (no fever, normal WBC)
    - Not repeating lactate after resuscitation to confirm clearance
