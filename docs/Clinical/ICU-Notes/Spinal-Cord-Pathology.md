## Basic Neuroanatomy and Function

### Ascending (Sensory) Tracts

**Spinothalamic Tract**

- Function: Pain and temperature sensation (contralateral)
- First order neuron → synapse in dorsal horn → second-order neuron crosses midline and ascends

**Dorsal Column (Medial Lemniscal Pathway)**

- Function: Fine touch, vibration, proprioception (ipsilateral until medulla)
- First order neuron ascends ipsilaterally → synapses in medulla → second-order neuron crosses and ascends to thalamus

**Other Ascending Tracts:**

- Spinocerebellar
- Spinoolivary

---

### Descending (Motor) Tracts

**Corticospinal Tract (Pyramidal Tract)**

- Function: Voluntary movement of contralateral side
- First order neuron (UMN): precentral gyrus, descends ipsilaterally via internal capsule → synapses in spinal cord
- Second-order neuron (LMN): exits to effector muscle

**Extrapyramidal Tracts**

*Commonalities: involuntary movement, muscle tone, facial expression muscle control*

| Tract | Origin | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Rubrospinal** | Red nucleus of the midbrain | Motor control |
| **Reticulospinal** | Reticular formation (pons and medulla) | Facilitation of voluntary and reflex responses, muscle tone, posture |
| **Vestibulospinal** | Vestibular nuclei | Lateral: muscle tone in leg extensors, posture; Medial: neck musculature, head movements |
| **Tectospinal** | Brainstem | Reflex posturing of head in response to auditory or visual stimuli |

## Nerve Anatomy

### Reflex Mnemonic

!!! tip "Remember Your Reflexes"
    **S1–S2** — Buckle my shoe *(ankle reflex, gastroc)*

    **L2–L4** — Kick the door *(knee reflex, quadriceps)*

    **C5–C6** — Pick up sticks *(biceps and brachioradialis reflexes)*

    **C7–C8** — Lay them straight *(triceps reflex)*

    **L1–L2** — Testicle move *(cremasteric reflex)*

    **S3–S5** — Winking drive *(anal wink reflex)*
