## Overview of Guidelines (by Recommendation #)

### Screening and Early Management

- **Rec 9:** Sepsis is an emergency and treatment should begin immediately
- **Rec 3:** For acutely ill adults en route to hospital by ambulance or flight, suggest using a standard sepsis screening tool over not using one
    - Speeds time to dx and overall treatment
- **Rec 21:** For adults with definite or probable sepsis and hypotension (septic shock) with anticipated time to hospital evaluation >60 min, suggest administering antibiotics in ambulance or flight
    - Prehospital abx should only be delivered after having established protocol for sepsis screening (as above)
- **Rec 2:** For hospitals and health systems, suggest using a "code sepsis" or "sepsis huddle" protocol
    - Screening process in place that flags patients as having potential sepsis
    - RRT or provider then evaluates if patient has sepsis and begins workup
- **Rec 10:** For adults with sepsis-induced hypoperfusion or septic shock, suggest administering **at least 30 mL/kg of IV crystalloid in the first 3 hours**
    - Frequent reassessments to avoid harm
    - Calculate based on **actual body weight** for normal/underweight patients
    - Calculate based on **ideal body weight** for obese patients (BMI >30)

## Infection

**ABX Timing:** Same as 2021 guidelines

**ABX Selection:**

- For adults with sepsis or septic shock: suggest **against** empirical antifungal therapy
    - Consider only if: immunocompromised, prolonged use of antibiotics or hospitalization, or intra-abdominal infection
- Recommend using **prolonged infusion of beta-lactams** for maintenance (after initial loading dose) over bolus administration — very strong recommendation

**De-escalation:**

- Evaluate for specific anatomical diagnosis or sources of infection that require emergent source control
- If source control needed: ideally within 6 hours (low certainty evidence)

---

## Hemodynamics

- **First-line vasopressor: IV Epinephrine**
- Target MAP >65
- Use invasive or non-invasive **dynamic measures** to guide initial fluid resuscitation over PE or static measures alone
    - Dynamic measures:
        - Passive leg raise
        - Pulse pressure variation
